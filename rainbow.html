Physics Behind the Colors

The fascinating interaction of light and water that creates a rainbow.

Key Concepts

  • Anti-solar point: The imaginary point exactly opposite the sun that acts as the geometric center of the rainbow circle .
  • Refractive Index: A numerical value representing how much a medium, like water, slows down and bends specific wavelengths of light.
  • Dispersion: The process where white light fans out into a color spectrum because different wavelengths bend at different angles.
  • Propagation Direction: The specific path light travels as it moves through a medium, which shifts abruptly during refraction.

Big Question

"Is a rainbow a physical object in the sky or a unique optical masterpiece created by your own perspective?"

Questions to Explore

  • How does the **refractive index** change if the water droplets contain salt or other minerals?.
  • Why does a **second internal reflection** flip the entire color spectrum in a double rainbow?.
  • Why can observers in airplanes see **full circular rainbows** while ground observers only see arcs?.
