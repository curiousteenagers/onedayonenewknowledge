The Golden Ratio in Nature

The golden ratio (φ ≈ 1.618) appears repeatedly in nature, art, and architecture. This mathematical constant seems to be nature's favorite proportion.

Key Concepts

  • Fibonacci sequence: A numerical series (0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5...) where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones, and the ratio between successive terms eventually homes in on the golden ratio.
  • Spurious correlations: A psychological trap where the human brain perceives a meaningful connection between two variables that actually lack a real causal link, often occurring because we are "hyper advanced pattern recognition machines".
  • Golden angle: A specific measurement of 137.5 degrees that allows plants to distribute leaves and seeds with maximum biological efficiency, ensuring they never perfectly overlap.
  • Extreme irrationality: The unique mathematical property of phi that makes it the most difficult number to approximate with a fraction, which helps prevent dangerous synchronization and resonance in complex physical systems.
