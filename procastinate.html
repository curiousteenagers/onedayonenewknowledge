Why We Procrastinate

Dreams have fascinated humanity for centuries. Science now shows they play crucial roles in memory consolidation, emotional processing, and creative problem-solving.

Key Concepts

  • Dopamine: A neurotransmitter associated with reward and motivation, often triggered by instant gratification.
  • Self-Regulation: The ability to manage emotions, behaviors, and impulses in pursuit of long-term goals.
  • Decision Fatigue: A decline in decision-making quality after making many choices over time.
  • Suboptimal Capacity: Operating below one's full mental or physical potential due to stress or unfinished tasks.

Vocabulary

Aversive (C2) Causing a strong feeling of dislike; unpleasant enough to make people want to avoid it.
Imminent (C2) About to happen very soon, especially something important or unpleasant.
Fleeting (C2) Lasting for a very short time.
Chronic (C1) Persisting for a long period of time; ongoing.
