History

Fall of Rome Myth or Fact

One of history's greatest collapses, surrounded by myths and enduring debates.

Key Concepts

  • Late Antiquity: A historical paradigm shift viewing the end of Rome as a period of cultural metamorphosis and recycling rather than sudden ruin [1].
  • Polytheism: The traditional Roman religious system where multiple gods were worshipped and the Emperor held a divine, sacred status [2].
  • Praetorian Guard: The elite military unit meant to protect the Emperor that eventually became powerful enough to assassinate him and auction the throne [3].
  • Feudalism: A social system born when wealthy Romans fled to independent country estates, accidentally tying peasants to the land for centuries [4].

Vocabulary

Big Question

"Was the end of the Roman Empire a sudden, violent demolition or a gradual evolution into the blueprint of modern Europe? [6]"

Questions to Explore

  • How does archaeological "hard data," such as lead levels in Arctic ice, prove that Rome's economy collapsed before the rise of Christianity? [7]
  • Why did the "geographic vulnerability" of the West make it a primary target for migrations compared to the fortified East? [8, 9]
  • In what ways did the "institutional metamorphosis" of late antiquity allow the Catholic Church to preserve Roman administrative borders? [1]
