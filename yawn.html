Biology

The Mystery of the Yawn

Dreams have fascinated humanity for centuries. Science now shows they play crucial roles in memory consolidation, emotional processing, and creative problem-solving.

Key Concepts

  • Gestation: The 12-week period when fetuses begin to yawn frequently in the womb. This early reflex occurs before breathing air, though fetal development is not detailed.
  • Arterial Blood: The fluid pumped into the head through the stretching of jaw muscles during a yawn. This acts as a mechanical pump for cooling without explaining circulatory physics.
  • Default Mode: The brain's passive state of daydreaming that a yawn forces into an active state. The source identifies this shift but does not detail the neural networks involved.
  • SSRI Medications: Anti-anxiety drugs that can cause excessive yawning as a biological side effect. While listed as a trigger, the specific pharmacological interaction remains unexplained.
