History

The Dancing Plague of 1518

A mysterious epidemic that compelled hundreds to dance uncontrollably.

Key Concepts

  • Hemophilia: A hereditary medical condition where blood does not clot normally, which afflicted the heir Alexei. Its secrecy allowed Rasputin to gain unprecedented influence over the imperial family's private decisions.
  • The February Revolution: A series of massive protests in Petrograd triggered by food shortages and war fatigue. It resulted in the sudden collapse of the monarchy and the establishment of a Provisional Government.
  • Bolsheviks: A radical Marxist faction led by Vladimir Lenin that seized power during the October Revolution. They were responsible for the final decision to execute the Romanov family to prevent a royalist restoration.
  • Ipatiev House: A merchant's residence in Yekaterinburg where the family was imprisoned under "special purpose" status. It remains historically significant as the site of their clandestine execution in July 1918.

Vocabulary

Incompetence (C1) Lack of ability to do something successfully or as it should be done.
Uprising (C1) An act of opposition, sometimes using violence, by many people in one area against those in power.
Abolish (C1) To end an activity or custom officially.
Legitimacy (C2) The quality of being legal or acceptable.
Unrest (C1) A state of dissatisfaction, disturbance, and sometimes fighting in a group of people.

Big Question

"Was the collapse of the Romanov dynasty an inevitable result of a flawed autocratic system or a preventable consequence of World War I?"

Questions to Explore

  • How did the perceived **incompetence** of Nicholas II as a military commander accelerate the army's desertion?
  • In what ways did the mystery surrounding Alexei's illness destroy the **legitimacy** of the crown among the Russian nobility?
  • Why did the **Bolsheviks** choose to eliminate the entire family line rather than keep them as political hostages?
