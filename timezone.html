How Time Zones Changed the World

Time zones were created to solve the growing need for global coordination. What began as local solar time evolved into a worldwide system that allows transportation, communication, commerce, and technology to function across continents.

Key Concepts

  • Greenwich Meridian: The 0-degree longitude line in London used as the starting point to divide Earth into 24 time zones.
  • Coordinated Universal Time (UTC): The world's primary time standard that remains constant and does not observe daylight saving changes.
  • Uniform Time Act of 1966: A federal law that standardized daylight saving time across the United States.
  • Solar Time: The traditional method of measuring time based on the Sun's position in the local sky.
