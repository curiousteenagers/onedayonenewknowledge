Biohacking

The science of optimizing your body and mind through lifestyle and technology.

Key Concepts

  • Nootropics: Cognitive enhancers or "smart drugs" used to improve memory and focus. They are mentioned as a staple of the biohacker toolkit, but the pharmacological impact on neurotransmitters is not explained.
  • Quantitative Self: The process of using wearable technology to collect personal health data. The video identifies it as the primary method for self-experimentation but does not explain how to analyze the resulting biometrics.
  • Nutrigenomics: The study of how diet affects gene expression and how genes influence our response to food. It is cited as a way to "hack" the metabolism, though the molecular biology behind gene signaling is not detailed.
  • Circadian Rhythm: The 24-hour internal clock that manages sleep and energy levels. While identified as a critical system to optimize for performance, the neurological mechanisms regulating these cycles are not explained.

Vocabulary

Big Question

"Is biohacking a path to superhuman performance or a dangerous disruption of our natural biological limits?"

Questions to Explore

  • How does the placebo effect influence the perceived efficacy of unregulated nootropics?
  • What are the ethical implications of using DIY gene editing kits outside of a laboratory setting?
  • How can long-term data tracking distinguish between genuine biological improvements and temporary physiological spikes?
