Philosophy

Ship of Theseus

A timeless paradox that challenges the nature of identity and change.

Key Concepts

  • Hylomorphic Dualism: Aristotle's philosophical theory suggesting that a human is a single unified substance where the non-physical soul gives continuous form and purpose to the ever-changing physical body [1].
  • Sorites Paradox: Also known as the "problem of vagueness," this logical trap challenges our ability to pinpoint the exact mathematical moment when a series of gradual changes results in a completely new object [2, 3].
  • Thomas Hobbes’ Twist: A 17th-century addition to the paradox where a second ship is built using the original rotting planks, creating a dilemma between an object defined by its continuous history versus one defined by its original material [3, 4].
  • Cellular Turnover: The biological reality that the vast majority of human cells regenerate and replace themselves roughly every seven years, making us materially different vessels over time [5, 6].

Vocabulary

Diligently (C1) In a way that is careful and uses a lot of effort.
Continuity (C2) The fact of something continuing for a long period of time without being changed or stopped.
Essence (C2) The basic or most important idea or quality of something.
Transformation (C2) A complete change in the appearance or character of something or someone, especially so that that thing or person is improved.
Ceaseless (C2) Something that never stops.

Big Question

"If every part of your physical and mental self eventually decays and is replaced, what is the 'thread' that anchors your identity through a lifetime of change?" [7].

Questions to Explore

  • How does John Locke's theory of **memory** as an anchor of identity attempt to solve the crisis of a changing body? [1].
  • Why does the **Star Trek teleporter** thought experiment challenge the belief that humans are merely "material meat computers"? [1].
  • In what way does viewing identity as an **ongoing narrative process** shift responsibility from what we "are" to what we "do"? [7, 8].
