Philosophy

Ship of Theseus

A timeless paradox that challenges the nature of identity and change.

Key Concepts

  • Ghost in the machine: A philosophical metaphor exploring whether a conscious soul or mind can truly exist within a mechanical structure like a computer.
  • Turing test: A classic experiment designed to determine if a machine can demonstrate intelligent behavior that is indistinguishable from that of a human.
  • Silicon chips: The physical hardware components made of silicon that serve as the fundamental building blocks for AI processing and computer memory.
  • Qualia: A term used by philosophers to describe the unique, subjective "feeling" of individual sensory experiences, such as the taste of an apple.

Vocabulary

Existential (C2) Relating to the state of being alive or the fact that someone or something exists.
Impermanence (C2) An innate quality or ability is one that you were born with, not one you have learned.
Accumulate (C1) The state of not lasting for ever or not lasting for a long time.
Agnostic (C2) Someone who does not know, or believes that it is impossible to know, if something is true.
Formidable (C2) Causing you to have fear or respect for something or someone because that thing or person is large, powerful, or difficult.

Big Question

"Is AI sentience a genuine scientific possibility or an "existentially toxic" illusion used by tech companies to drive stock prices?"

Questions to Explore

  • How does human empathy trick our brains into projecting personhood onto software that simply predicts words?
  • Why is there an "absurd contrast" between our concern for AI rights and our treatment of living organisms like prawns?
