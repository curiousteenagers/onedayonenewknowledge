Why Do We Forget Things?

Forgetting may seem like a flaw, but neuroscience shows it is an essential function of the brain. It helps us filter information, adapt to new experiences, and make room for what matters most.

Key Concepts

  • Highly superior autobiographical memory (HSAM): A rare condition where individuals remember nearly every day of their lives with extreme detail.
  • Synaptic plasticity: The physical process where nerve fibers and synapses alter to store long-term information.
  • Forgetting curve: A timeline illustrating the rapid decay of information, showing that over half of new knowledge is lost within an hour.
  • Memory palace: An ancient mnemonic technique that maps complex data onto a familiar physical location within the mind.
