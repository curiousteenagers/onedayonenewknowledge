Journey of Sound

Follow the incredible path of sound, from tiny vibrations in the air to the signals your brain transforms into music, voices, and everyday sounds.

Key Concepts

  • Malleus, incus, and stapes: These are three microscopic bones in the middle ear that act as a mechanical lever system. They amplify physical sound vibrations before they reach the fluid in the inner ear.
  • Auditory nerve: This is the neural pathway that carries electrical impulses from the ear to the brain. It is responsible for the final stage of translating physical vibrations into perceived sound.
  • Hertz (Hz): A unit used to measure the frequency of sound waves per second. Humans can typically hear a range of frequencies between 20 and 20,000 hertz.
  • Inferior colliculus: This is a midbrain region where auditory signals and high-frequency touch vibrations converge. It allows the brain to process sound and tactile information simultaneously.
