Key Concepts

  • Commodities: These are physical goods like gold, oil, or grain mentioned as assets that tend to grow in value alongside inflation.
  • CDs (Certificates of Deposit): A type of financial product suggested as a "smart move" for long-term saving to protect cash from losing value.
  • Federal Reserve: Identified as the central bank or "monetary police" of the United States responsible for managing the money supply.
  • Real Estate: Mentioned as a long-term investment asset class that serves as a defense against the devaluation of currency.
