Why we feel Deja Vu?

Déjà vu has intrigued humanity for generations. Science now reveals it as a memory misfire where the brain's recognition systems fall out of sync, serving as a vital tool for error detection and rapid pattern recognition.

Key Concepts

  • Dual Processing Theory: A cognitive framework where fast, automatic systems and slow, conscious systems fall out of sync, causing a brief illusion of familiarity
  • Spandrel: An evolutionary byproduct or unintended side effect of having a complex brain that prioritizes fast pattern recognition over perfect accuracy.
  • Temporal Lobe Epilepsy: A neurological condition involving localized seizures that can manifest as intense, frequent, or prolonged episodes of déjà vu.
  • Frontotemporal Dementia: A brain disorder affecting memory and familiarity centers that may lead to pathological déjà vu as a symptom of cognitive decline.
