Philosophy

The Trolley Problem

A famous ethical dilemma that challenges our moral decision-making.

Key Concepts

  • Substrate Independence: The philosophical idea that consciousness is not tied to biological matter. It suggests that a sufficiently complex silicon program can possess genuine subjective consciousness if it replicates the human brain's structure.
  • Ancestor Simulation Trilemma: A logical argument by Nick Bostrom stating that one of three paths must be true: we go extinct before maturity, civilizations lose interest in simulations, or we are almost certainly living in one.
  • Base Reality: The original, non-simulated physical universe made of atoms and quantum waves. It is contrasted with simulated worlds made of digital bits and computational math.
  • Posthuman Stage: A technologically mature era where a civilization has the computing power to run billions of "ancestor simulations" using less than a millionth of a planet-sized computer's capacity.

Vocabulary

Indistinguishable (C2) Impossible to judge as being different when compared to another similar thing.
Trivial (C1) Having little value or importance; in this context, something that requires almost no effort or resources.
Replicate (C2) To make or do something again in exactly the same way.
Inadvertently (C1) Not intentionally; by accident.
Facilitate (C1) To make something possible or easier.

Big Question

"If our subjective experience is identical in both worlds, does the physical composition of our universe actually matter for a meaningful life?"

Questions to Explore

  • How does the concept of substrate independence challenge the traditional "biological" definition of the human soul?
  • Why does even a tiny rate of continuous technological improvement eventually lead to a statistical probability that we are simulated?
  • Can the current distribution of VR headsets be seen as the first step toward building the exact simulations Bostrom described?
