How Are Fjords Created?

The geological forces behind the formation of deep, narrow coastal valleys.

Key Concepts

  • Orogeny: The violent horizontal collision of tectonic plates that crumples the Earth's crust to form massive mountain ranges.
  • Eperogyny: A slow vertical movement that gently lifts or sinks large, rigid blocks of the Earth's crust over millions of years.
  • Subduction: A geological process where a denser tectonic plate is forced underneath another and sinks into the Earth's mantle.
  • Endogenic Processes: Internal geological forces, driven by the heat of the Earth's core, that build up the terrain through movement and pressure.
